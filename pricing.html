How pricing scales

Pay for visibility that matches real usage.

The product is easier to adopt when the billing model stays concrete. TokenLens emphasizes tracked requests first, then uses retention and collaboration features to separate plans.

Primary metric

Tracked LLM events or requests

Request-based pricing is easier for teams to understand than model-specific token billing and maps directly to the work TokenLens performs across providers.

  • Free: 25k events per month
  • Pro: 1M events per month
  • Team: 5M events per month
Secondary levers

Retention, projects, teammates, and workflows

As production usage grows, customers typically need more history, more projects, shared reviews, exports, and alerting. Those needs should increase predictably without turning the pricing page into a spreadsheet.

  • Longer raw and aggregate history
  • More projects and team members
  • Alerts, exports, and operational review tools

FAQ

Questions teams ask before moving from evaluation to production.

Why price by tracked events instead of tokens?

Tracked events are easier to understand, work across providers, and map more cleanly to the product's core usage model. Token-based billing can remain an internal cost signal without being the main customer-facing meter.

Do all plans include the dashboard?

Yes. Every plan includes the core dashboard for requests, tokens, latency, cost, errors, provider breakdowns, and model visibility. Higher tiers expand scale, retention, and operational workflow support.

What usually triggers an upgrade?

Most teams upgrade when they exceed the event allowance, need longer history for incident review, or want alerts, exports, and broader team access around production monitoring.

Is the product optimized for large enterprise governance first?

No. The initial product framing is practical observability for developers. Governance-heavy packaging can come later, but the first value is understanding usage, latency, errors, and cost quickly.

Next step
